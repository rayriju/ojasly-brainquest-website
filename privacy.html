04

Parental Consent

Before any data is collected, we require verifiable parental consent. By law (COPPA, GDPR-K, DPDP), we will not collect any data about a child without this verified consent. If we discover consent was not properly given, we will delete all associated data immediately.

1
Parent/guardian provides their email address (not the child's)
2
Parent/guardian reviews this Privacy Policy and accepts our Terms of Use
3
Parent/guardian checks the consent box confirming they are the legal guardian
4
Consent timestamp and parent email are stored securely in our database
05

How We Store & Protect Data

🏗️

Storage

All data is stored in Supabase, a SOC 2 Type II compliant cloud database.

🔐

Encryption

Data is encrypted in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest (AES-256).

👥

Access Control

Only authorised personnel with a business need can access data. All access is logged and audited.

🗂️

Retention

Data is retained for as long as the account is active. Parents may request deletion at any time.

🌏

Data Region

We store data in ap-south-1 (Asia Pacific — Mumbai).

06

Data Sharing

✅
We do NOT sell, rent, or trade children's data. We do not share data with advertisers, analytics companies, or third-party marketers.

We share data only in these limited cases:

  • Supabase (infrastructure provider) — bound by data processing agreements
  • Legal authorities — only when required by law, court order, or to protect safety
07

Your Rights as a Parent or Guardian

You have the following rights at any time:

📋

Access

Request a copy of all data we hold about your child

✏️

Correction

Ask us to correct any inaccurate information

🗑️

Deletion

Request deletion of all data — actioned within 30 days

🚫

Withdraw Consent

Withdraw at any time; we will stop processing and delete data

📤

Portability

Request your data in a machine-readable format where applicable

✉️
To exercise any right, email ojasly.brainquest@gmail.com with the subject line: 'Parental Rights Request'.
We will respond within 30 days and verify your identity before actioning any request.
